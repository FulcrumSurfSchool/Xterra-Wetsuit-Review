Xterra Wetsuit Review

Posted by Dan Mori

As a Fulcrum Surf coach, it is very important that we use a reliable suit. During the winter, we find ourselves with clients that are more of an intermediate surf level and therefore sit still in the line-up instead of pushing and being more active in the white water. Our suits need to keep us warm, flexible and durable. All suits are warm when they are brand new and you might brag that you have the best suit. Will you have the same enthusiasm after 6 months? Most wetsuit brands lose their warmth after 3 months. Trust us, we know because it’s torture for us if the suits are not warm while we teach lessons in the winter. An ocassional set comes and you surf the wave to impress your client and when you’re limited from all that 4/3mm rubber, you tend to hold back. Having a loose suit is imperative for surfing. After all you are out there to surf and be free. So you have two out of three checked out but the third priority is that your wetsuit can last a full season or even two seasons. After 4 months or so, you will notice on most suits that the seams start to fall apart and the blob of glue wears off and starts to cut into your skin and gives you rashes. The calf area splits open or your shoulder area starts to open up. Don’t let this be happen to you! Xterra wetsuit is a large brand that can buy premium rubber in bulk resulting in a lower price point. They also have an amazing customer service and will service suits right here in San Diego, California. Let us give you the right feedback.
